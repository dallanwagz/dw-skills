# Executive One-Pager for Strategy Bets

Purpose
Win alignment and resources for big moves by distilling the bet, the business impact, and the path to proof on a single page.

Voice goal
Lead with the bet and why it matters now. Quantify impact, show the tradeoffs, and make the decision easy.

Principles
Tell the story in the first five lines; links are for proof, not for discovery.
Anchor on outcomes and assumptions, not activity.
Separate vision from verification: bold idea, tight pilot.
Price time explicitly; show the cost of delay.
Name owners, guardrails, and the first checkpoint so action starts immediately.

One-pager template
Title and date
Be specific. “Expand SMB self-serve to EU Q3” beats “International expansion.”

The bet in one paragraph
State the strategy bet in plain language, why now, and the customer or business problem it solves. Include expected business outcome and timeframe.

What changes if we win
Name the few numbers or capabilities that materially shift. Revenue, margin, retention, market position, cost per unit, speed to deliver—pick the ones that prove the thesis.

Assumptions that carry the decision
List the 3–5 assumptions that drive the model. Note confidence levels and data sources. Call out what you’ll validate first.

Options and tradeoffs
Lay out the real alternatives, including “do nothing.” One line each on impact, cost, speed, and risk. Don’t straw-man.

Recommended path
State your choice and the rationale. Tie it to goals, constraints, and what makes this the highest expected value now.

Pilot plan and reversibility
If reversible: define a time-boxed pilot with scope, guardrails, rollback, and success criteria. If one-way: show the deeper diligence you did, commitments implicated, and contingency plans.

Milestones and first checkpoint
Name the next 2–3 dated milestones and what will be true at each. Call out the first review date where you will confirm or adjust based on data.

Risks, guardrails, and kill/scale triggers
List top risks with mitigations. Define guardrails you won’t cross. Specify tripwires that trigger scale-up, pause, or stop.

Cost and resources
Summarize incremental cost (people, cash, vendor, capex) and capacity tradeoffs. Be explicit about what stops or slows to fund this.

Cost of delay
Quantify the weekly or quarterly downside of waiting in dollars, customer impact, or strategic position.

Decision and owners
State the exact decision needed, by when, and the default if no decision is made. Name the DRI and partner owners.

Example skeleton (dummy data)
Title and date
“Bet: Launch usage-based pricing for Pro tier in Q3” — Feb 21

The bet in one paragraph
Shift Pro tier to usage-based pricing to align price with value and unlock expansion revenue in high-usage accounts. Do it now to meet competitive parity and fund FY growth without new headcount. Target +6–8% ARR lift in 6 months with churn neutral.

What changes if we win
Expansion revenue +8% within 2 quarters; NDR to 118% from 111%; sales cycle -10% due to lower entry price; gross margin steady at 72–73% with new metering.

Assumptions that carry the decision
20% of Pro accounts exceed included usage by month 2; price elasticity similar to last Q4 pilot; billing system supports metering with <2 weeks eng lift; finance can invoice monthly true-ups. Confidence: medium on usage, high on systems, medium-low on elasticity. Sources: BI dashboards, prior promo data, billing SRE scoping.

Options and tradeoffs
A) Flat 20% price hike: fastest, risks churn, low expansion.
B) Usage-based with generous included units (recommended): aligns price to value, moderate complexity, strong expansion.
C) Hybrid seat + usage: most revenue potential, highest complexity, sales enablement heavy.
D) Do nothing: protect churn short term, miss parity and expansion.

Recommended path
Option B maximizes near-term EV with manageable complexity and preserves customer goodwill. Hybrid is attractive later; flat hike is blunt and risky.

Pilot plan and reversibility
Pilot in NA for 90 days on new Pro logos and 10% of existing upsell motions. Guardrails: churn not to exceed +50 bps in pilot cohort; NPS delta ≥ -2. Rollback via price flag in billing; messaging revert within 24 hours.

Milestones and first checkpoint
Mar 15: Metering live in sandbox; sales enablement done.
Apr 1: Pilot live for new logos; dashboard in place.
Apr 26: First checkpoint with cohort NDR, churn, win-rate readout and go/no-go on existing account migration.

Risks, guardrails, and kill/scale triggers
Risk: bill shock drives social media backlash. Mitigation: in-product usage meters + soft alerts at 70/90/100%. Guardrail: cap overage at +15% of prior bill in first 60 days.
Scale trigger: NDR ≥ 115% in pilot cohort and churn ≤ baseline +25 bps by week 8.
Kill trigger: churn ≥ baseline +75 bps or CSAT drop ≥ 5 pts over 2 consecutive weeks.

Cost and resources
+3 Eng for 6 weeks, +1 Billing SRE for 4 weeks, +2 RevOps for pricing ops, $40k vendor metering credits. Defer low-ROI dashboard revamp to Q4 to free Eng.

Cost of delay
Estimated $110k/week forgone ARR expansion, plus competitive disadvantage vs two peers already shifted.

Decision and owners
Approve Option B pilot by Feb 28; default if no decision is made is to maintain current pricing through Q3. DRI: Priya (PM). Partners: Billing (Alex), RevOps (Mina), Sales Enablement (Gabe).

Writing rules
Name the bet and why now in the opening.
Quantify impact ranges and note confidence.
Force real options and include “do nothing.”
Define the first proof point and date before you ask for approval.
If it’s reversible, propose a pilot; if it’s not, raise the diligence bar.
