# Delegation and How to Work With Me

## Purpose
Delegation is the process of multiplying your impact by distributing outcomes to capable owners. It is a critical skill for scaling leadership and fostering team growth. A "How to Work With Me" document acts as an operating manual, clarifying expectations and communication styles to eliminate guesswork and friction.

## Core Principles

### 1. Delegate Outcomes, Not Tasks
Focus on the "what" and "why" (the outcome) rather than the "how" (the task). Providing outcomes with guardrails allows individuals to think critically and take true ownership.

### 2. Scale Through Delegation
Avoid becoming a bottleneck. Every decision that flows through a leader limits the organization's ability to scale and the team's ability to grow.

### 3. Auditing Is Not Micromanaging
Delegating doesn't mean disappearing. Regular auditing—asking questions, reviewing work, and catching risks early—is essential for accountability without hovering.

### 4. Connect Delegation to Growth
Align delegated outcomes with the individual's growth goals and capabilities. Meaningful ownership is the primary driver of professional development.

## Delegation Framework

### Step 1: Know Your Team
Understand each person's strengths, growth edges, learning styles, and track records.

### Step 2: Identify What to Delegate
- **Keep**: Strategic decisions requiring your authority, performance management, and key high-level stakeholder relationships.
- **Delegate**: Project leadership, execution within guardrails, stakeholder updates, and problem-solving outcomes.

### Step 3: Frame the Conversation
Open with the desired outcome and its strategic importance. Define clear guardrails (time, budget, authority limits) and establish a check-in cadence.

### Step 4: Establish a Check-in Cadence
- **High-risk/First-time**: Weekly or twice-weekly.
- **Medium-risk/Proven**: Weekly.
- **Low-risk/Proven**: Bi-weekly or monthly.

### Step 5: Audit Effectively
Ask questions rather than providing answers. Offer suggestions as possibilities, not demands. Use details to maintain a clear picture of progress and risk.

## "How to Work With Me" Template
A living document for your team that covers:
- **Communication Style**: Preferred channels (Slack, Email, Sync) and response expectations.
- **Decision-making**: Which decisions you want to be involved in vs. which you trust the team to make.
- **Values & Expectations**: What you prioritize (e.g., radical candor, speed, quality).
- **Availability**: Your focus time, "open door" hours, and how to handle urgent escalations.
- **Feedback**: How you prefer to give and receive feedback.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Delegating Without Trust**: Building trust through small wins must precede full delegation.
- **Lack of Guardrails**: Failing to set constraints leads to misalignment and frustration.
- **Swooping/Rescuing**: Taking work back when it gets difficult prevents team growth.
- **The "Dump"**: Delegating unwanted work without connecting it to the person's growth.

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Outcome is clearly defined and strategically linked.
- [ ] Guardrails (authority, resources, time) are explicit.
- [ ] Person chosen for the task is a good match for their growth goals.
- [ ] Check-in cadence is established and understood.
- [ ] "How to Work With Me" document is current and shared.
- [ ] Committed to auditing without micromanaging.
